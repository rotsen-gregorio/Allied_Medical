Vocational Nursing Program

Launch Your Nursing Career

Comprehensive practical training for nursing fundamentals, clinical skills, and patient support. Complete 1,564 hours of intensive theory and clinical training to become a Licensed Vocational Nurse.

Call to Enroll

Course Objective

The Vocational Nursing course is designed to teach students extensive and in-depth knowledge and skills necessary to perform various duties including medication administration, treatments, and other procedures as nurses caring for patients in long-term facilities or acute care hospitals.

Upon completion of 1,564 hours of theory and clinical training, students will be evaluated by the school on their competency to be eligible for graduation and to qualify for the licensing examination.

Licensure is awarded by the State through the Bureau of Vocational Nursing and Psychiatric Technician (BVNPT) after passing the Competency Examination (NCLEX).

Program Overview

Vocational Nursing is divided into theory class (604 hours of lecture) and clinical/skills laboratory training (960 hours of practical experience), totaling 1,564 hours for a year and a half.

Theory Class Subjects

  • Nutrition
  • Psychology
  • Growth & Development
  • Fundamentals of Nursing
  • Nursing Process
  • Communication
  • Patient Education
  • Medical Surgical Nursing
  • Communicable Disease & Gerontological Nursing
  • Rehabilitative Nursing
  • Maternity Nursing
  • Pediatric Nursing
  • Home Health Nursing
  • Leadership & Supervision

Admission Requirements

Completion of the following are required before starting the program:

  • Passing the entrance examination for Writing, Reading and Numerical Skills (fee applies)
  • 17 years or older
  • US High School Diploma/GED Certification/Equivalent (evaluation needed for foreign transcripts)
  • Copies of Driver's License & Social Security Card
  • Two (2) 1x1 photographs
  • Nursing Assistant Certificate/License
  • Anatomy & Physiology
  • Pharmacology
  • CPR "C" Card
  • First Aid & Fire Safety Card
  • Medical Clearance (Physical Exam, Hepatitis titre A & B, Varicella, Rubeola, Rubella, Drug Test, TB)
  • Letter(s) of recommendation (minimum of 2) from identifiable and respectable US citizen for at least 1 year, or California resident for at least 2 years

Job Opportunities

As a Licensed Vocational Nurse (LVN), you will be eligible to work in the following areas:

  • Acute Care Hospitals
  • Transitional Unit Facilities
  • Skilled Nursing Homes
  • Nursing Registries
  • In-Home Care Services
  • All other Allied Health Institutions

Note: AMHS currently does not have job placement services, but our graduates are highly sought after in the healthcare industry.

State Licensing Requirements

A license is required for Vocational Nurse practice in the State of California. Upon program completion, graduates may apply for the licensing examination given by the Board of Vocational Nursing and Psychiatric Technicians.
